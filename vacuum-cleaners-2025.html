Best Vacuum Cleaners for 2025

We lab-tested 18 robot, stick, and upright vacuums released for the 2025 cycle to find standout models for hardwood, carpet, and pet-focused households. Here’s how the top performers stack up across suction, automation, and ownership costs.

2025 headline pick: Dyson V17 Detect Absolute

Dyson’s newest flagship keeps the brand’s high-end price tag, but backs it with 12% stronger sealed suction than last year’s V15 and a dual roller head tuned to alternate between hardwood and low pile carpet without swapping tools. The on-board particle sensor now color codes allergens in real time, a boon for parents managing indoor air quality.

  • Up to 78 minutes of runtime with the bundled long-life battery pack.
  • Self-diagnosing filter system with app alerts for clogs and maintenance.
  • Wall dock doubles as a trickle charger and accessory organizer.

Robot vacuum champion: iRobot Roomba Vision Pro

Vision Pro brings adaptive pathfinding powered by a new depth-sensing front camera and on-device Tensor chip that identifies spills versus solids. In our obstacle course it avoided cables 98% of the time while matching last year’s S9+ in suction tests.

Why it wins

  • Self-emptying base with HEPA filtration rated for 60 days of debris.
  • Room-specific cleaning schedules triggered by Home Assistant automations.
  • No-go zone setup from a 3D map instead of flat floor plans.

Budget upgrade: Shark Stratos Lift-Away IQ

At under $399, Shark’s Stratos line blends a sealed HEPA system with odor neutralizing pods and a detachable canister for above-floor cleaning. During our carpet lift test it pulled 92% of debris in a single pass—only five points behind Dyson’s V17 at nearly half the price.

  • Dual brushroll head transitions between deep pile rugs and hardwood.
  • LED edge lighting reveals dust in baseboards and under furniture.
  • App-enabled maintenance reminders and voice assistant start commands.

Buying advice for 2025 households

Two trends matter this year: smarter automation and better allergen control. Households juggling hybrid schedules should lean on robot vacuums with obstacle recognition and multi-floor mapping so runs can trigger while you’re away. For pet owners, prioritize models with sealed HEPA systems rated for 0.3 micron capture and replaceable odor cartridges.

Expect ongoing software updates too—Dyson and iRobot now push firmware quarterly, while Shark’s Stratos series receives filter life tracking through the app. Plan for routine maintenance like brushroll cleaning and dustbin washing to keep suction consistent.
